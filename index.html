My Manifesto

My ideas here are only a starting point for the change and support we can implement together at our Students’ Union. I strongly welcome any ideas you might have so don’t hesitate to get in touch!

How to support well-being from the perspective of yourself and loved ones who want to help!

I understand a lot of people want to do their best in supporting their loved ones with their mental health and well-being. If elected I will work closely with the exec team to organise sessions- both online and in person. These sessions will be support groups which will bring to light different techniques to help their loved ones from activities organised by us.

I’m aware of the services the SU and university itself offers from Advice service, department well-fare officers and a few more. In terms of managing your own well-being I plan to implement a variety of elements to tackle different types of our well-being. This includes, physical well-being I would love to with UoM sports to offer some more free de-stress sessions, mental well-being with anxiety support groups, managing mental illness and finally student well-being which is more specific to ensuring student life style remains exciting with going out and trying new things, but draw an educational aspect to how to look after our peers especially with in relation to the spiking and sexual assault cases that were brought to light this year and more directly, support groups for anyone who identifies as LGBTQ+.

Selfcare week

Implementing sessions which will help you distress and manage things like anxiety throughout the year and not just during the exam period as it currently stands. I will work my hardest to have a week specifically dedicated to this in each semester. And hold fortnightly self-care events on a wide spectrum as I understand self-care means different things for different people and I’d love to welcome ideas and listen to what you would like to see (and maybe even try them out myself!).

Student meet ups

Every 8-10 weeks I’d like to implement a randomly chosen focus group to talk to for 30 minutes to hear their thoughts and opinions on their experience as a student and work towards what they say.

Understanding certain difficulties LGBTQ+ students face, I would like to organise the same focus group idea but specific to focusing on their needs and hearing their voice.

The exec team are always welcoming students to speak to them, but I believe inviting us students face-to-face where possible shows we’re here and we want to listen to these issues you might be facing and be a friendly person to talk to!

I want us to not just be listened to, but also heard. If elected, I will be YOUR well-being and liberation officer.
